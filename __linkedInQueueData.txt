The CFPB may be reconsidering its prior guidance that recognized EWA as a better alternative to more costly loan options.  In doing so, it will have to walk back the pro-EWA treatment from 2017 when it carved some EWA progarms out of the Small Dollar Lending rule and 2020 when they exempted some EWA programs from TILA. 

There's no reason to change course now.  EWA lacks the characteristics of credit and shouldn't be treated as such. And, even if it is credit, optional fees to expedite a transaction have never been finance charges (another requierment to be TILA credit). The CFPB is warranted in continuing to study EWA and its impact on consumers, but wedging it into inapplicable credit rules isn't the answer.
